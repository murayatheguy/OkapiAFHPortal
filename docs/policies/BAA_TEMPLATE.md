## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is an Adult Family Home subject to HIPAA; and

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides electronic health record and practice management services; and

WHEREAS, Business Associate will create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information on behalf of Covered Entity;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises below, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

Terms used but not defined in this Agreement have the meanings set forth in HIPAA, including:
- "Protected Health Information" or "PHI"
- "Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"
- "Breach"
- "Security Incident"

---

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

### 2.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only:
- As necessary to perform services under the Service Agreement
- As required by law
- For Business Associate's proper management and administration
- To provide data aggregation services (if applicable)

### 2.2 Safeguards

Business Associate shall:
- Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards per HIPAA Security Rule
- Ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI
- Protect against reasonably anticipated threats
- Ensure workforce compliance

### 2.3 Reporting

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity:
- Any Security Incident within 24 hours of discovery
- Any Breach within 24 hours of discovery
- Any use or disclosure not permitted by this Agreement

### 2.4 Subcontractors

Business Associate shall:
- Ensure subcontractors agree to same restrictions
- Obtain written assurances from subcontractors
- Maintain list of subcontractors available upon request

### 2.5 Access to PHI

Business Associate shall:
- Make PHI available to individuals per 45 CFR 164.524
- Make PHI available for amendment per 45 CFR 164.526
- Provide accounting of disclosures per 45 CFR 164.528

### 2.6 Government Access

Business Associate shall make internal practices and records available to HHS for compliance determination.

### 2.7 Minimum Necessary

Business Associate shall limit use, disclosure, and requests for PHI to minimum necessary.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

Covered Entity shall:
- Provide notice of privacy practices limitations
- Notify of authorization revocations
- Notify of restrictions on uses or disclosures
- Not request actions that violate HIPAA

---

## 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

This Agreement is effective on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

### 4.2 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate if the other materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 30 days of notice.

### 4.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:
- Business Associate shall return or destroy all PHI
- If return/destruction not feasible, protections extend
- Business Associate shall certify destruction in writing

---

## 5. BREACH NOTIFICATION

### 5.1 Discovery

A Breach is discovered on the first day known to Business Associate or any employee, officer, or agent.

### 5.2 Notification

Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity within 24 hours of discovering a Breach, including:
- Nature of the Breach
- Types of PHI involved
- Individuals affected (if known)
- Steps taken to investigate
- Mitigation measures
- Corrective action planned

### 5.3 Covered Entity Responsibility

Covered Entity is responsible for individual and HHS notifications.

---

## 6. INDEMNIFICATION

Business Associate shall indemnify Covered Entity for damages arising from Business Associate's breach of this Agreement or negligence.

---

## 7. MISCELLANEOUS

### 7.1 Amendment

This Agreement may be amended only in writing signed by both Parties.

### 7.2 Interpretation

This Agreement shall be interpreted consistent with HIPAA.

### 7.3 No Third-Party Beneficiaries

Nothing in this Agreement creates third-party beneficiary rights.

### 7.4 Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Washington.
