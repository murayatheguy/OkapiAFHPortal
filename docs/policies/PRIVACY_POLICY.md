## 1. Introduction

Okapi Care Network ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of our users and the residents they serve. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information through our Adult Family Home management platform.

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Information You Provide

**Facility Owners:**
- Account information (name, email, phone)
- Facility details (address, license number, services)
- Staff information
- Billing information

**Care Staff:**
- Name and contact information
- Credentials and certifications
- PIN for authentication

**Families:**
- Name and contact information
- Relationship to resident

### 2.2 Protected Health Information (PHI)

For facilities using our EHR features, we process PHI including:
- Resident demographics
- Health conditions and diagnoses
- Care plans and assessments
- Medication records
- Daily care documentation (ADLs)
- Incident reports

**PHI is processed under HIPAA Business Associate Agreements with covered entities.**

### 2.3 Automatically Collected Information

- Device information
- IP addresses
- Browser type
- Access times
- Pages viewed
- Audit logs

## 3. How We Use Information

### 3.1 Business Purposes

- Provide and maintain our services
- Process transactions
- Send service communications
- Respond to inquiries
- Improve our platform
- Comply with legal obligations

### 3.2 PHI Use

PHI is used only for:
- Providing EHR services to facilities
- Supporting treatment, payment, and healthcare operations
- As required by law
- As authorized by the individual

We do NOT use PHI for:
- Marketing
- Sale to third parties
- Any purpose not disclosed in our BAA

## 4. Information Sharing

### 4.1 Service Providers

We share information with service providers who assist our operations:
- Cloud hosting (Railway, Neon)
- Payment processing
- Customer support tools

All service providers are bound by confidentiality agreements and, where PHI is involved, Business Associate Agreements.

### 4.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by:
- Law or legal process
- Government request
- To protect rights and safety
- In connection with merger or acquisition

### 4.3 PHI Disclosures

PHI is disclosed only:
- To the facility (covered entity) per BAA
- To individuals about their own PHI
- As required by law
- With valid authorization

## 5. Data Security

We implement safeguards including:

**Technical:**
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256)
- Multi-factor authentication
- Role-based access controls
- Audit logging

**Administrative:**
- Workforce training
- Access management procedures
- Incident response plan
- Regular risk assessments

**Physical:**
- Secure data centers
- Access controls

## 6. Data Retention

- Business data: Retained while account is active
- PHI: Retained per HIPAA requirements (minimum 6 years)
- Audit logs: Retained 6 years

See our Data Retention Policy for details.

## 7. Your Rights

### 7.1 All Users

You may:
- Access your account information
- Update your information
- Request account deletion
- Opt out of marketing communications

### 7.2 PHI Rights (via Covered Entity)

Individuals have HIPAA rights exercised through their care facility:
- Access to PHI
- Amendment of PHI
- Accounting of disclosures
- Restriction requests
- Confidential communications

## 8. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

## 9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy periodically. We will notify users of material changes via email or platform notice.
